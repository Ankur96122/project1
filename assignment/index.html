About ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the space agency of the Government of India, headquartered in Bengaluru, India. Established in 1969, ISRO has been a pioneer in space exploration and satellite technology, making India a global leader in space science.

From launching satellites for communication, Earth observation, and navigation to achieving interplanetary missions, ISRO's progress in space exploration has been remarkable. ISRO's missions include the Chandrayaan series to the Moon, the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan), and the upcoming Gaganyaan mission, which aims to send Indian astronauts into space.

Key Achievements

Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission)

ISRO became the first space agency to successfully reach Mars on its first attempt with the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) in 2013, making India the first country in Asia to reach Mars.

Chandrayaan Missions

PSLV-C37

In 2017, ISRO's PSLV-C37 rocket launched a record 104 satellites in a single mission, setting a new benchmark for space agencies worldwide.

Future Missions

ISRO has several exciting missions lined up for the future, including:

  • Gaganyaan - India's first crewed space mission, with plans to send astronauts into space by 2024.
  • Chandrayaan-3 - A mission aimed at landing a rover on the Moon's South Pole.
  • Aditya-L1 - A mission to study the Sun's outermost layer.
